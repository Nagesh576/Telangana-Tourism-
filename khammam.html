Khammam Fort

Khammam Fort, also known as Stambhadri Fort, is a magnificent hill fort that stands as a symbol of the region's rich history. Built in 950 AD by the Musunuri Nayaks, the fort was later strengthened and expanded by the Kakatiyas, Velamas, Qutb Shahis, and Nizams. Spread across 4 square kilometers on a granite hill, the fort features massive granite walls, bastions, and grand gateways like the Khilla Darwaza and Raathi Darwaza. Inside, one can find ancient temples, the Khilla Masjid (mosque), a palace built by Nawab Shoukat Jung, and the Zafar Baoli â€” an old stepwell used for rainwater harvesting. The fort's architecture is a blend of Hindu and Islamic styles, reflecting the diverse rulers who governed it. Now protected by the Archaeological Survey of India, Khammam Fort offers panoramic views of the city and hosts cultural events like the Stambhadri Sambaralu, drawing visitors and history enthusiasts alike.

Lakaram Lake

Lakaram Lake is a popular tourist spot and urban getaway located in the heart of Khammam, Telangana. Once a natural water body, the lake was developed by local authorities into a scenic attraction for recreation and relaxation. Surrounded by well-maintained parks and walkways, Lakaram Lake offers a refreshing atmosphere for visitors, especially during the evenings. The lake is known for its boating facilities, with paddle boats and motorboats available for hire, allowing visitors to enjoy the calm waters. The adjacent Lakaram Lake Park features a children's play area, landscaped gardens, and seating areas, making it ideal for families and couples. The lake is beautifully illuminated at night, adding charm to its serene surroundings. Local vendors around the lake offer snacks and refreshments, making it a lively spot. Lakaram Lake has become a favored destination for both locals and tourists seeking a peaceful escape within the city's busy environment.

Wyra Reservoir

Wyra Reservoir is a picturesque water body located near Wyra town in Khammam district, Telangana. Built in 1930 during the Nizam era, the reservoir was designed for irrigation and drinking water supply, benefiting thousands of acres of farmland and nearby villages. The dam stretches about 2.35 kilometers with a height of 27 meters, controlling water flow through its spillways. Surrounded by rolling green hills, Wyra Reservoir offers a peaceful and scenic environment that attracts visitors for picnics, fishing, and boating. Recently, speedboat rides and small boating facilities have been introduced, adding to its charm as a local tourist spot. The serene landscape, fresh air, and calm waters make it an ideal destination for families and nature lovers seeking a relaxing getaway close to Khammam city.
